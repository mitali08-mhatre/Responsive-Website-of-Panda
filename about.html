About Panda Life

Introduction

Panda, also known as Giant Panda, are a rare species of bear native to the mountains of central China. They are known for their distinctive black and white fur and their love for bamboo, which makes up over 99% of their diet.

Habitat

Panda primarily live in temperate forests high in the mountains, where they thrive in cool, moist environments with abundant bamboo. They are most commonly found in the Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu provinces of China.

Conservation Efforts

Due to habitat loss and low birthrates, Panda were once on the brink of extinction. However, concerted conservation efforts, including protected reserves and breeding programs, have helped increase their population. As of recent counts, there are over 1,800 Panda in the wild.

Fun Facts

  • Panda spend up to 14 hours a day eating bamboo.
  • Panda are excellent tree climbers and swimmers.
  • Baby Panda are born pink and weigh only about 100 grams at birth.
